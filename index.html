Five models, one dataset

The data below are generated from a known equation, with no measurement error at any point. The true mechanism is therefore known with certainty — a condition never available experimentally. Fit the five standard models to it and read the correlation coefficients. Then change the coordinate system, and change nothing else.

Each model is fitted by ordinary least squares in the linearised coordinates from which its rate constant is conventionally extracted, and R² is reported in that same space — the procedure used in the published literature, and the one that produces published R² values. Q∞ is taken as known (100% of dose). For first-order over the full curve, ln(0) is undefined at complete release, so the final point is dropped and the fit uses nine points. Cost curves are computed analytically from each model's own fitted parameters; no cost curve is fitted to anything. Accompanies the article and Supplementary Material S1 (derivations and verification).
